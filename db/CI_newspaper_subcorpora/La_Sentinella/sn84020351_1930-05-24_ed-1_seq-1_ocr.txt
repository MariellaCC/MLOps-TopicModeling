THE POWER OF SYNTHESIS
By Dr. Joseph Pantaleone
If we look very closely at a painting by a great artist we shall
lose the idea which the artist meant to portray. The picture will
then appear as a meaningless mass of different colored splotches
and daubs of paint. We may say that in looking at the picture in
this way we are regarding it analytically i. e. we are concentrating
and examining each part separately.
But we are not getting the effect which would be produced
by putting all the parts together at once we are not looking at
the picture as a whole we are not looking at it synthetically.
Now I have purposely taken this example to bring out a
point which I think will be of great practical value to us all. It
seems to me as if most people look at life analytically rather than
synthetically. Their minds are merely occupied by looking at
life's aspects one by one they do not see the relation between
the parts and as a result they lose thè thrilling and inspiring view
of life's mighty picture seen as a whole. But how can we see the
entire world at once? How can we picture it synthetically to our
minds without actually viewing it concretely? With the mind's
?ye we can all view the world synthetically we can all see its
nfinite panorama, its magnificent tragic and inspiring spectacles,
its historic and dramatic past,, and even its more marvellous
future. The trouble with most of us is that we lack the imagin
ation to see the world synthetically. It is in part due to this power
of synthesis or imagination that the world's greatest minds have
been able to achieve so much and derive so much "confidence and
inspiration from within themselves.
The love poetry, history and science one must have a
highly developed and keen imagination which consists in the
ability to keep before the mind at the same time many details and

: hough t constantly in mind we would soon be acting much
differently. Let me give you an example only of thousands I

If we all stopped to think a minute we would see how short
human life is how vast and how mysterious the world is, what

as lack the ability to sense these truths and to realize tre pro

the truth which the Bible proclaims when it says that man is built

of our vast stores of potential
which lie within each one of us.
