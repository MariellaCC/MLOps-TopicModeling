RÙRAL PROSPERITY
In former years the business men of cities were often rather
indifferent to the country people and disposed sometimes to make
fun of them. But during recent years they have discovered that .
their prosperity depends largely on the extent to which prosperity
exists in the nearby country towns.
What we need in such, a city as Bridgeport is to have every
home in all our outlying towns prosperous and happy, so that its
people will be able to contribute to the community welfare, and
to create a market for the merchandise distributed in tfriogeporx.
An abandoned or poorly worked farm in our territory is a
damage to the whole section. It means resources lying idle, labor
not fully employed, and a decay of social forces. '
Several things are necessary for this rural prosperiti". Good
- oads are very essential. People dislike to live in a rural neighbor-,
hood where the rdads are poor. - -
Good schools are also a necessity. Just as soon as a family
has children, it begins to consider education as a factor in its
location. If the schools in the country neighborhood are not good,
that family is likely to go elsewhere. . ,
Business methods on the farms are vitally necessary. Agricul
ture is a scientific business calling for technical skill. It is carried
-n by knowledge handed down from a past generation, it is not
i:vely to be prosperous. The farmers need lo follow the develop
ments in their line as closely as ,a manufacturer has follow the
"hanges in his styles. The business men of a district need to look
after the facilities that are offered to the farmers so they can
train themselves in modern methods. If such facilities are not
being offered, the business man should take the initiative to see
that they are provided.
