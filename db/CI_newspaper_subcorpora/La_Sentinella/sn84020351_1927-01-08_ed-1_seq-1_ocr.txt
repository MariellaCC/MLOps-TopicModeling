LIFE AND ITS MEANING '
"We are born and we sojourn here for a time. A new year is
ushered in and its span of life is run. Association are formed
beatiful frienships and then comes the parting, the passing on,
and the cornine of the new. So it is witH life.- We are born,' and in
the springtime we plan and build for the future. Then comes tht
summer of life, when we are watching the beauty that surrounds
us and wondering if the harvest will be all that we hppe for. Then
the afternoon of life, or in other words, the fall, when our fondest
hopes are realized or shattered. We cannot go. back and plant
anew; we must harvest what we have sown, and then by the
harvest we will be judged. And then comes the evening of life, or
winter, when we look back over, profitable or unprofitable life and
review, just as we do today, the things that have taken place, the
worth-while and the failures. And by this record we will be judged.
So the year ends, just like a life ends. A year seems short; a
life is short, and we must crowd a lot into that life if we expect
to leave for posterity something that will make the world a little .
better. If we are going to leave behind pleasant memories, we
must of necessity render some service to humanity. : V
As the year is brought to a close, another takes its place, and
we go on planning just as vigorously as of old. This gives us
beatiful thought that life does not end at the grave. If al1, our
hopes and .ambitions were centered in one year there would be
little in the way of accomplishment or profit accruing to humanity.
And so it is with life; if we lived with idea that everything was
swallowed up in death, that there was nothing beyond the grave;
no future, the whole thing would be a sham." - . .

do not get much personal return, as the boy may not do much for

they put into it, just the same as with a boy. A lot of folks are'
always kicking because their towns do not grow fast enough, when
they have done nothing to feed that town with effort and spirit
thatcreate community growth. '
