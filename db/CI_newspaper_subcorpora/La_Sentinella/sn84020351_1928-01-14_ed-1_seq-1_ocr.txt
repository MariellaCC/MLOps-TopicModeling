HE HAS THE KIND OF COURAGE BUSINESS MEN NEED
When Col. Lindbergh told a few of is intimate associates that
he had decided to fly over Paris . thev told him in suhstanr that
he was crazy, that he had better wait for more favorable weather
conditions, etc., etc. ; but after Lindbergh had assured himself that
the "Spirit of St; Louis" was as near perfect as mortal man could
make it, he had the courage to say, "I am going!" and he did.
When this same young man, a few weeks ago, went down to
the field one morning, he said, "I am going to Mexico City!"
and he did. ,
There are two kinds of courage. One kind is typified by the
man who ignorantly takes unwarrented chances, trusting to luck;
the other kind is that which this grand young man, Lindbergh,
has m abundance a courage backed by intelligent, thoughtful
preparation. ' k -
We have entered a new year. While it is a continuation, it is,
nevertheless, a beginning, a starting over a road where we will
undoubtedly encounter obstacles as did Lindbergh; but while he
gazed across the seas, while he dreamed and hoped for success,
his courage was based on the confidence he had in "We" in his
ship and in himself. . .
As you enter upon this year, men, my hope is that you may
be imbued with the kind of courage that enable Lindbergh to be
come master of conditions, instead of permitting conditions to
master him. In other words, fear not! Place your trust in "We"
and the percentage will be larger in your favor.
