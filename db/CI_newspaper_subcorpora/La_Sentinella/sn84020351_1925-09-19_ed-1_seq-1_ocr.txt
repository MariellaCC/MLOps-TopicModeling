DONT LET THEM REMAIN STRANGERS
It's mighty nice to be able to identify every face you see
down-town but it's not an evidence of city growth. It's ple'as
ant to know everybody you meet but if you know them ail, it's
a sure sign the city's population is not increasing.
Have you noticed the large number of "strange faces ori the
downtown streets of Bridgeport lately? They're there. "'Aria the
longer you have resided in Bridgeport, the greater impression the
newcomer will make upon you. Of course, you can't call 'them all
by name; maybe you don't even speak to them yet. But the
sooner you do, the sooner we old timers get acquainted with the "
strangers, the sooner we extend them the right hand of civic
fellowship and make them one of us, the more solid and perma
nent will be our growth as a city.
Bridgeport is proud of it's strange faces these days; these
new-comers have adopted Bridgeport, and are going to help us
build it into a bigger and better city. Let's not let them for long
feel themselves, strangers.
