"Là Sentinella" is urged in a letter from a reader to "take up
the cudgel for decency". Bathing-girl revues and nude advertising
come in for particular excoriation.
We don't wonder, that various individuals are becoming un
easy but why shift the responsibility to the press? After all,
charity, decency, and everything else that figures in steadying
civilization, properly begins in the home.
Mothers and fathers may well exclaim at bathing revues -
and junior bathing revues conducted miles from any beach or
water. They may hold up their hands in horror at the extent of
skin exposed by their daughters to the public gaze. They may well
deride the insolent styles and customers of drug-store johnnies.
But why pick on the public print, which is for the most part only
a reflection of readeV interest?
V'La Sentinella" has always stood will always continue to
stand for clean news and articles; for everything that makes
for wholesome home life. But it cannot undertake the futile task
of doing a job that belongs to the parents.
We'll return the favor we'll toss the callenge right back
where it belongs, into the Hands of the heads of families. The are
the ones directly responsible for the warpingj of society and the
loss of ideals. Complacent mothers have all too often looked on and
smiled while daughters have simperingly displayed their charms
unhampered by excess clothing. Misguided and misguiding fathers
have roared their endorsement othe brute man as against the
man of gentility ancL intellect Ederle, Ruth,-,Grange-Jégri
and Tunney rule the newspaper because the subscribers want
them to. , ' ... '

could not by any amount of campaigning rid the human heart of
its devels. Conscience works from within.

ELECTRICITY AND THE WORKING MAN
It is in the field of industry and labor that electric ligth and
uower has madp its most marked advances and extended its
J service to the greatest degree, according to William Green, Presi
' dent of the American Federation of Labor, who says: "The lives
, and welfare of the great mass of working people associated with
! industry have been vitally affected through the introduction and
. use of electricity in industry.
"The use of machinery in industry antedated the use of elec
tricity. A great change was brought about through the introduct
ion and installation of machinery of various kinds in pratically all
lines of industry. During this period of important changes capital,
labor and machinery were regarded as the three factors essential
to production.
"With the andvent of electric power another factor was ad
ded. This factor increased the value of machinery many fold and
the productivity and efficiency of the individual worker was in
creased in ratio to the increase in value. Electric power has proved

its general use, it. has proved that the wages of wage earners can

was so far-reaching that it made an appreciable difference in the

A DEPENDABLE FARM CROP
The sugar beet is one of the most dependable farm crops in
Western states. Due to the policy of the big sugar refining com
panies, there is always a sure market with a minimum price fixed,
and the farmer who puts in a few acres of a sugar beets is sure
of his tax money and spare change for the winter.
There is no better sugar than that made from beets and the
industry should be fully protected by our govenment from outside
interference. Without proper protection, sugar factories would
become idle, and an industry which supplies bread and butter for
thousands of farmers and workmen, would be nipped in the bud,
and our nation left entirely dependent on foreign sugar supplies.
