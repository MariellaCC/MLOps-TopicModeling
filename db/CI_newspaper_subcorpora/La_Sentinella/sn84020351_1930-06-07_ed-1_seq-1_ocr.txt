PREMIER MUSSOLINI
Benito Mussolini, Italy's Premier addressed a crowd of
150,000 workers in Milan, May 25, telling them that discipline of
the laboring classes "firmly united around the symbol of the
lictor's rods" is the element that is elevating Italy's economic
situation above that of other nations.
Cannon kept firing salvos in honor of II Duce's visit, throwing
varicolored confetti over the crowd's heads. A powerful radio
outside the courtyard filled the big park with national and
Fascist airs.
"It is unnecessary to recount what the Fascist Government
has done for labor. We think of your interests, all your needs,
because we love you as workers and fellow-Italians," the Premier
said.
Previously the Premier had attended a gathering in Balilla
Stadium before 35,000 youngsters.
Before a seething crowd of Fascists the day before, packed
into the great square before the famous cathedral, Mussolini spoke
with defiance for Fascism's enemies, and made a call for a cele
bration here in 1932 of the second decade of the revolution.
Some spectators said there were 300,000 gathered to hear
lì Duce's speech. The Fascist leader had some difficulty in
controlling the excitement of his auditors who broke out into
shouts of "Down with France" several times. Mussolini silenced
them with a gesture.
He said alleged reports of the debarkation of Italian troops
in Albania were "absurd".
Commemorating Italy's entrance into the war 15 years ago,
and recalling her 652,000 fallen, he declared "We do not comme
morate this date to revive old hatreds, because we are reconciled
fully end loyally with our enemies of yesterday, and even linked
hi sincere friendship with some of them".
He warned the Blackshirts not to let the people "go to sleep
before the baaing of lambs that are really wolves, so that they
may not tomorrow wrake up before one of the more or less tragic
surprises of history."
He hotly denied that Italy's economic situation was bad, and
said it was not worse than that of any other country.
"Some people would like to see me make a ministry of the
eld style, but they forgot that there is no longer a ministry, but
r. regime formed, not of parliamentary groups but by the bloody

period of struggle. I am the chief and the creator of this regime

informed of what is being prepared elsewhere and of the dominant
spirit among certain of our neighbors."

HELPING BOYS
"No man stands so straight as when he stoops to help a boy."
It is such an easy matter to become careless and pay no
attention to boys. Men are busy with the affairs of business.
Worrying details occupy one's thoughts. Yet, all about us, are
)oys who need help the help that comes from an encouraging
void, a helping hand, a little boost. All these helpful things take
nit just a minute of any man's time, and yet they mean so much
Lo a boy.
It does not take long for a boy to grow and develop into a
nan. It may be that what we may do today will have a big
influence on what kind of man he will be. Are we doing the thing
Lhat will help the boy? Just a cordial word of greeting in the
lorning as the boy is on the way to school will" frequently give
the lad just the needed encouragement for that particular day.
Do we do that? Can we truthfully say that we have been the
means and channel by which a boy gets inspiration to do better
things.
There is so much we can do in boys' work without even
having to take any time to do it. Most men are willing: to do this
if they only realized how easily it can be done.
