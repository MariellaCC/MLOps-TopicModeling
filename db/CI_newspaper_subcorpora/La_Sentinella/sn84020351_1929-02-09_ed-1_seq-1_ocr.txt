The Nature of Courage
By Dr. Joseph Pataleone
vecchio libro di legge in Joseph Cubelli; Tex Congressman B. O Sullivan:
Abraham Lincoln
There are various types of courage. The highest courage is
the ability to face the most perilous danger for the sake of a lofty
ideal or a noble cause. When Philippe Pinel in the nineteenth
century endangererd his life by advocating a more humane treat
ment of the insane, he was exemplifying this type of true courage.
In those barbarous days all mental disease was thought to be due
to demons and evil spirits, and the unfortunate patients were
horribly tortured, put in chains and dark dungeons and bet cruelly
in order to drive the witches and devils out of their bodies. In a
age of the darkest ignorance and superstition, Pinel had the
audacity to suggest that insanity was due to a disease of the
brain, and that the patients should be treated as" sick persons.
rather than be punished as culprits possessed by evil spirits. By
this highly courageous action Pinel was taking his own life in his
hands, and at the same time heMvas giving to history one of its -
noblest exemples of true courage. Because of his ideas Pinel was
persecuted by the people of his day and his life was at times in
great danger. But it was through his heroic courage and that of
several other pioneers in psychiatry, (Tuke, Valsalva Chiarugi,
etc.) that our modern conception of insanity as a disorder of the
brain has been evolved and that our wonderfully humane asylums
and hospitals for the insane have been developed. How much'
suffering has been eliminated! What a blessing to millions of. un
fortunate beings was Pinel's heroic courage!
Again when Walter Reed allowed himself to be bit by the
mosquito of yellow fever in order that science might study this
disease and when he subsequently died from the infection and the
knowledge . which this experiment gave science, furnished the
means to conquer this dread malady he was exemplifying what
may be called the highest form of human courage.
When Christ died on the cross, when Columbus set forth on
his venturesome journey toward the unknown, when Lindbergh
ilew to Paris, all were exemplifying the highest form of human
courage.
There are thousands of heroes of science, art and reform
whom the masses have never heard of and it is pitiful to think
hat most people know more about the ephemeral popular heroes
of the day than they do of the world's greatest men whose work
in comparison is of infinitely greater value to humanity as a whole.
What a contrast there is between true courage and that of
dare-da vils who merely indanger their lives for no reason what-"
ever except to amuse'" and thrill a morbid crowd. t Human life -is
iacred and how sacretr,it can be made we have just seen hy the
above example of some of the world's most glorious heroes; Any
man who endangers his life carelessly without a really noble cause
in view, is sinning against himself and against the whole world.
or he is depriving himself of the joy and privilege of living for
a worthy cause whilst he is deying the world the fruits of .his
abors m commendable mterprise.
What is the secret of courage? It is really quite simple.
Disregard and forget the danger involved that is what psycho-
egists tell us fix your mind firmly and solely on the end to be
achieved. If you are going to jump over a ravin or a chasm, the
only way to jump successfully is to forget the chasm,- with its
dangers, ana the possibility of failure, for just as soon as you
allow yourself to think of danger, of fear and of failure just as
surely wilhyou fail! This is a cardinal principle of psychology
it is the law of auto-suggestion, which no thinking person can
litlord to disregard m his everyday Me. Forget the danger and
act that is the whole secret of courage!
There is a great joy in living dangerously but one must
know how, otherwise the living might be very brief. Men are
afraid to take chances in life and that is why persons of rare
ability very often turn out to be failures. The man who plays safe -
in life a game, rarely gets very far. We must have couracre and -
know how to make our courage go farther than that of the other "
ellow. Timidity end fear are very great deterrents and the sooner -
we get rid of them better it will be for us.
Let us never forget that if we are normal individuals, our
brain and ability is just as good as that of any other person
in fact if we knew how to use our normal gifts properly, scientists
.ell us we could all become great successes but the. skillful use
cf courage must ever remain a great essential in playing the game
of life.
Lack of courage) shows lack of insight into the true-nature
of our existence. Life is continually dangerous every second in
spite of ourselves. Any moment and without previous notice we
arre all suject to any one of a million possible fatal accidents,
whether we sit in the parlor, sleep in a bed, or make , a transatl
antic flight. Why then should we fear anything whatever? .
Courage comes with knowledge of ourselves and of life in
general and it is a natural outcome of general wisdom. Courage
comes from faith and faith means to understand immortality and
death itself, and to know that life is fundamentally benevolent
toward its beings, that life is eternal and infinite and that it is
always getting bettere and better. Faith tells us that mankind is
progressing and that ultimately justice, love end truth will trimph!
To fight against overwhelming odds and evils, as has been done
by the world's heroes in all ages, is the truest courage and highest
faith combined. j
Courage we have said is essential to success. Courage is
essential even in love. To the bold go the spoils of life, and thè love
if the fairest. The brave life's chosen sons in whom she his well
satisfied and takes great delight. '
Courage is of the stoic it lends poise and balance to the
.-oul. Courage is of the young end it keeps the heart young. He
who practises courage is closely acquainted with a good deal of
happiness. Courage developes the more we practise it. More
courage comes to him who has courage 'and thus fufilled an ex
ample of the law which says that to him who has, it shall be added
to, to him who has not, it shall be taken away from. Courage
deals with the uncertain and thus gives us a real thrill the
human mind is always fascinated by the uncertain, and courage
surely funishes this element of doubt which so characterizes all
human life.
Progress is always due to the skillfully used courage of the
few. All our commodities and conveniences were due to the tact
ful eourage of the few. Every change in the Universe of Life has
involved courage on the part of its beings or intelligent forces.
Let us in all our fields of action, whether it be in religion,
philosophy, education, science, art, business or invention apply
our highest courage, for only by courage can men free themselves
from the House of Bondage of which the Old Testament speaks.
Progressive evolution in all the spheres of life has been made
possible by courage, and courage will point the way to higher
things still till the day this world will be made a heaven.
Uptopia can only be achieve by courage (and by faith). All honor
then to courage, the Transformer of the world I ..
