FOLLOW THE LEAD
There is at present a condition in San Francisco that is more
or less general throughout the country, and one that is coming
increasingly to the front in politics and business the approach
of a climax in the old conflict between a municipally owned and
privately owned street railway system. The privately : owned line
the "Market Street Railway" is giving good service and paying
the city many thousands of dollars in taxes. The tax-free munici
pal railway also gives good service, but differs in that it is not
paying its way, and is removing from the treasury tax funds
placed there by the privately owned line.
The San Francisco Bulletin editorially adopts a non-partisan
practical attitude and calls for an expert survey of the situation
removed entirely from the hand of politics. And here it blazes the
way for papers in cities that face the same situation, whether
with street railway, water plants, gas and electric systems or
other municipal undertakings outside the function of government.
In the great majority of instances it has been conclusively
shown that municipal ownership will not work, mainly, because
of politics, and that private ownership makes for prosperity, for
wardeness, honesty and economy. The result of this has been the
almost wholesale selling of municipal electric power and railway
noldings to private corporations. And now, in cities such as San
Francisco, where there is any question, there can be but one
solution ah expert survey, untouched by bias or politics.
