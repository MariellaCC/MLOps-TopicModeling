WALKING ON PAVED ROADS.
This often happens:
A pedestrian on a paved country road walks on the pave
ment near the right side, at night.
A car conies up behind him. The pedestrian walks calmly
on, under the impression that the driver of the car can see
him plainly and will keep from hitting him.
Another car comes from the other direction. It has
bright lights which dazzle the driver of the first car. He may
not see the pedestrian at all. The man on foot in is grave
danger of being struck.
The man on foot, as well as the driver of the car, should
never take anything for granted. In order to avoid accidents,
both parties to the potential accident should be keenly alert all
the time.
It would seem that one way of avoiding this contingency
is for the pedestrian on country roads to walk against the traf
fic on the left side of the road.
