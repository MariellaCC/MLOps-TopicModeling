NO MORE HOME DRUDGERY.
American inventive genius seems bent on driving drudgery
out of the home, reducing the housewife's labor to .a minimum.
No longer' is she required to debate between going out for an
afternoon's enjoyment or staying at home to cook dinner. She
can switch on the automatic oven and go out for the afternoon,
knowing that dinner will begin to cook at four o'clock and stop
cooking at five-thirty, or whatever time of the day she wishes.
No longer does she need to worry how she will get along with
out her kitchen maid. She has but to turn on the right attach
ment and at once she has an electric servant that can mix dough
for cakes and bread, batter waffles and pancakes, cut shortening
into pastry, mash potatoes, jnix mayonnaise, beat eggs and icings,
whip cream and meringue, grind coffee, chop meat, nuts and
raisins, strain soups, purees and sauces through a fine sieve.
There is also a colander for applesauce, prunes, cottage cheese
and spinach. This electric servant chops or slices vegetables,
chips the ice for its own three-quart freezer, turns the freezer
and indicates when the contents are frozen.

LIST.

prompt reward for faithful services rendered. This can come
only thru prompt payment of your doctor's bill and a kindly
word for the good work they dò. We have said it before and we
say it again. "The doctors are the worst imposed upon set of pro
fessional men known', but the happy part of it is, these servants

else from whom you obtain service or goods. Pay your doctors

ROUND AND SQUARE.
A square peg never fits a round hole. This adage is as old
as man almost, yet its application is not always apt. The trouble
is not with the peg but with the hole. We say a man is as square,
as a brick; that he stands four-square to every proposition; that
he is square in all his dealings. The square from time immem
orial has been emblematic of rectitude. On the other hand, the
circle, while emblematic of infinity, is also symbolic of , those
phases of charcater that are not always desirable. Thus it is
better to be a square peg than a round hole, better for you and
all concerned.
