THE LAW OF INDEPENDENCE
By Dr. Joseph Pantaleone
The nature of every living being leads it to look out primarily
for its own welfare. The law of egoism dominates the entire
animal kingdom and most men are subject to it. Rare indeed is
the generous soul who looks out for his neighbor's well-being
above his own. And so in a world in which the vast majority of
living organism are only interested in themselves, it is only the
fool who leaves to others the carrying out of important details
pertaining to his own prosperity, comfort, and general well-being.
The old wisdom holds: the best way to do a thing is to do it
yourself.
The satisfaction and joy of knowing that all our achievements
and comforts were procured by our own efforts is very inspiring
and produces the finest type of character in a man. It makes him
stand on his own feet.
One must develop one's character by doing things for himself.
No one else can eat, breathe, or sleep for you and so if you want
wisdom, happiness, or success, there are certain things which you
must do yourself and which no one else can possibly do for you.
In other words, he who expects to be a man of the world must
be a go-getter and must not leave important details for others to
do for him. He certainly won't be developing his abilities to their
full extent by using this method of shifting the burden, and
furthermore, the job will either not be done at all or in most cases,
it will be badly done. The trend in modern education today is
toward letting the student think and do things for himself. Te
development of initiative and the application of the law of indepen
dence in thought and action are being more and more recognized
by our leading educators.
The progress of humanity through the centuries has been a
beautiful example of the law of independence. Man has had to
fight his own battle all by himself against the grim overpowering
forces of Nature. Alone he had to face the infinite against tre
mendous odds and he won !
Alone are all beings born and' alone must each face his
ultimate dissolution. Life is a solitary adventure each must
work out his own salvation and since each one of us presents a
different problem, the solution is always different in each case. It
is this which makes all true education so difficult.
The struggle for existence is keen in the modern world, but it
is preeminently a solitary struggle. Each individual openly is
fighting the other in the grand scramble for supremacy! Woe to
the weak! Woe to those who have things made easy for them!
He who recognizes and applies this principle of independence in all
his actions and who early learns to do everthing for himself,
always seeking the arduous path rather than the easy one will in
the end far outrun his competitors and find himself triumphantly
acclaimed as a conquering hero by all those who possess the
deepest wisdom.

SAFE DRIVING
There ought to be a standard course of instructions for new
drivers, in which they would be thoroughly drilled in the vital
requirements of safe-driving. Ninety-five per cent of all motoring
accidents are caused by human delinquency. Careful drivers of
well-balanced mentality and normal physical endowments seldom
figure in motor-car accidents. If the public could be educated to
the necessity of perpetual watchfulness against accident the
casualities of the streets and the highways would show a gratify
ing decrease.
