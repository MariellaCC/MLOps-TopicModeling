THE SCHOOLS AND COMMUNITY PROGRESS
Parents are frequenaly urged to take an interest in the public
schools of their city, as if they were the only ones that had ani'
special concern with them. But every interest in the city is affect
ed by them. Unless a city has well developed schools, it is
regarded as unattractive as a residence town and people will avoid
such a place if they can. ;
Hence the business men and the wage-earners and the
farmers and all elements should take a keen interest in school
questions. They should pay taxes cheerfully for the best the city
can afford. They should talk school problems over with teachers
and parents, and form intelligent opinions here in Bridgeport as
to the needs of our schools, and what can be done to make them
ptill more useful.
