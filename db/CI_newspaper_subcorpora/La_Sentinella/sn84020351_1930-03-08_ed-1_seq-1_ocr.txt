Nel 1830 Mazzini si affiliò alla Società dei
Carbonari che congiurava segretamente per la
liberazione d'Italia dal giogo straniero. Perse
guitato dalle spie riparò in Francia ove organizzo
una società politica sotto il nome di "Giovane
Italia" e dopo vari anni di peripezie passate fra
speranze, ansie, disinganni, tradimenti e nuove
speranze, Egli si trovò esiliato a Londra.
Ma Giuseppe Mazzini aveva dedicata tutta la
sua anima alla grande causa della redenzione con
una fermezza ed una fede che avevano del sublime
e cosi i moti rivoluzionarii del 1848 lo riportarono
in Italia, ove unitamente a Garibaldi favori, po
sponendo la politica alla Patria, Re Carlo Alberto
nelle sue lotte contro l'Austria.
Nel Febbraio del 1849 Giuseppe Mazzini si
trovò a Roma dove era stata proclamata la Pepub
blica e dove, nel Marzo successivo, con Aurelio
Maffi ed Armellino, vi formò il famoso triumvi
rato. Ma dopo una strenua difesa di Roma contro
le armi francesi, caduta la città nelle fatali gior
nate del Luglio, il Mazzini andò nuovamente in
esilio. .
Nel 1857 Egli propose un'altra insurrezione
nell'alta Italia e finalmente cooperando con Gari

THE IDEAL CITIZEN
An ideal citizen is the one who sees clearly and ever some
thing good in the community and never loses the opportunity to
spread that good news abroad that others might derive thè
benefits therefrom, whose unselfishness prompts him to want
others to share opportunities, health and pleasure which his home
city offers.
Loyalty is the first requisite for the ideal citizen. The love
for the place and his neighbors which dismisses the thought ff
self interest or policy and resolves itself into the knowledge 6$
duty when he does all in his power to make the city a better place
in which to live. v " -
There are three distict attitudes which a citizen may assume
toward the 'place in which he lives, which furnishes him food,
shelter and association for himself and family, to boost, to remain
quiet or to knock. The booster is that ideal citizen who is never
forgetful of the obligations which is due the home city. He is
ever at the front when any movement is launched which, might
tend to the advancement of the interest of the city. While others
see darkly, to him good is visible at all times. The quiet citizen
is satisfied to let the neighbor do the work and content to lull the
city asleep and let it rest. The knocker is the man who is without
a country. There is no room for him anywhere, his presence
dampens the enthusiasm of every man or group of men who-"
unfortunately come in contact with him. Having no faith in his
own ability to go forward, he naturally lacks faith in his home
town to do so.
You have the chance to belong to either of the three classed
If you are a booster, your neighbor knows it well and will boost
you. If you are aligned with the! quiet class, you will hot bf
regarded one way or the other, for the man who selects to steer
in the middle of the stream, gets no support from either side.. If
unfortunately you are a knocker, get ready to be knocked, fo
eventually it is coming to you. As you give, so will it be mètea
out to you. "
Bridgeport would be an ideal city if only there were more
ideal citizens. Let's have them.
