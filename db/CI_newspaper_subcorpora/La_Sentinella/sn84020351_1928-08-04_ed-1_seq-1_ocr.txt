CLEAN JOURNALISM PAYS
A wonderful tribute is being paid to Adolph A. Ochs, of
Chattanooga, Tennesse, on the 50th anniversary of his ownership
of the Chattanooga Times. This great newspaper man, famous
publisher of the New York Times, has made his success on the
theory of publishing "all the news that is fit to print".
If newspaper are tempted to place first emphasis on crime
and scandals they should reflect that newspapers of that type
are not pleasing to the substantial people who spend the bulk of
the money. If a newspaper is to be valuable as an advertising
medium, it has to please the man and woman who are the heads
of families and control family expenditure. Those folks do not
want papers that deal mostly with the seamy side of life.
YOUR PAY ENVELOPE
It is a Receipt in Full for One Week of your Life?
What do you find in your pay envelope each week?
"Money", you say, but there is more than that.
Did you ever stop to think of it and to ask yourself "Is
this a receipt in full or is there an unpaid and, at present, un
payable balance due on this week of my life that -will pay
dividends in the future."
A working life of thirty years which is longer than the
average - has 1,500 weeks to be sold.
How are you selling your life? ; -j"
How did you sell the week just gone?
Look at the bill you have drawn from your envelope.
Ask yourself "Is this payment in full, or on accont?"
Those bills represent your full market value today.
Your employer pays all you worth, but you are not taking all
he pays.
Each day every week you reject the opportunity to
learn more to develop your ability to increase your worth.
Look again at your envelope. Make yourself see with clear
eyes the opportunity to make next week influence all the weeks
that are left in "your 1,500.
It is up to you whether you take a receipt in full or an
account for that precious, unrecoverable week of your life!

HOME TOWN NEWSPAPER
The home newspaper as an institution, has survived many
difficulties; the onslaughts of opposition, the increase in cost of
publication, the perii of unpaid subscriptions, the uncertainty of
advertising patronage, the problems and the hazards of its own
mistakes. Through all this the home newspaper still lives. The
newspaper is a growth a development . made possible by
the co-operative and receptive spirit of the people. It is the echo
of the community's rights and a direct avenue for contact between
the people of the community.

WHO WALKS ANY MORE?
The number of people who walk to their work in the mornings
could be transported on a hand car, but by the time the stores are
open and ready for business the streets are filled with parked
autos. Most of the walking done in America today is in following
the golf ball or hopping across a dance floor.
