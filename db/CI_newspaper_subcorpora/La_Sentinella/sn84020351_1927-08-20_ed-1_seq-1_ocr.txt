PRESIDENT COOLIDGE
"I do not choose to run for president in 1928." With this
brief expression, President Coolidge has stunned the party leaders
and left-them in a quandary. - V
There has been a great deal of talk about a "third term" and
much speculation as to what would happen in the next national
convention of the Republican party ; but it seemed to be generally
understood that President Coolidge not only could have nomination
but would have it without much doubt.
There is a great deal of difference between, "I do not choose
to run" and "I would not accept the renomination", or if the
President wanted to make it especially emphatic, he might have
sid, "Under no circunstances will I consider the acceptance of
nomination next year."
What 'is meant by the President's brief statement? Opinions
differ, but our guess is that the President means what hi says,
that he does not "Choose to run" but that if he finds sentiment
favorable he will consent to run and will so announce himself at the
right time. If that guess is correct, then the statement may be
regarded as a "feeler" of the public pulse. The President is a close
observer and he understands politics thoroughly; We know he
means it when he says he does not "choose to run," but that
doesn't mean that he will not accept the nomination if it is
tendered. -

STALLING ON THE CROSSING
In many cases where automobiles are hit' by rairoad trains,'
ine report tells how the car stalled on the crossings. Why is it that
cars seem so much more likely to stall there than anywhere else?
The answer, no doubt, in in most cases, that the driver slowed
down a good deal to listen for trains, and so lost his momentum
that the car unfortunately stopped right on the tracks. Some will
say it is better to keep going at full speed and get over as soon as
possible. ' . . . V ' '. '
If such a driver had gone down into a lower speed, his chances
of stalling would have been very much less. It only takes a minute
to, use proper care in going over a crossing, and that minute may
save your life and the lives of your passengers.
