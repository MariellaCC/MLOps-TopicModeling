THE BIBLE
A preacher asks: "Do newspaper men read the Bible? If so,
why do they read it?"
To which Arthur Brisbane replies: "They do, nearly all
of them. Here are a few reasons for the reading:
"Religion is the oldest, most powerful institution for the help
of mankind. Newspapers are among recent institutions for public
service.
"Every newspaper man needs the Bible, churches need the
useful publicity of newspapers, and receive less of it than is their
due. A writer or editor should know the English language. The
Bible is the best teacher of English.
"Job and Isaiah could do more to correct the deplorable weak
ness and carelessness of newspaper English than all other teachers
combined.
The chapter in which Job curses his day contains energy
enough to supply 40 solemn editors for a month.
"The beauty and power in Isaiah will pull any tired writer
from his rut of repetition, making him realize what power there is
in words.
"Newspaper men read the Bible because it supplies them with
j arguments, complete, convincing, not to be answered.
! "Hevwood Broun wishes to rebuke an Episcopalian gentleman
who expressed a desire that Negros go to some other church. He
found his rebuke in the Bible.
"Christ did not say: "I am the light of the white world," or
"Suffer little Caucasian children to come unto me," or " Love thy
Nordic neighbor as thyself."
"Read the Old Testament, with its magnificent power for
j inspiration, the New Testament for consolation.
I "In the Bible, as in all things, there is improvement with time.
The Old Testament emphasizes the anger of a "jealous God" who
reminds His erring people, "Vengence is mine, I will repay .
"What a change to the Sermon on the Mount, preached by a
divine spirit, to whom all men, even miserable sinners, were as
brothers.
"Even science, . mistakenly called religion's enemy, finds
strength in the Bible.
"Newspaper men, other men, women and children read the
Bible or should read it, because not to know it is vulgar ignorance.
It is neglect of the most important record of man's struggle to
answer, through faith, questions important above all, that cannot
be answered elsewhere.
The foregoing, we submit, is one of the finest sermons to
appear in modern times.
