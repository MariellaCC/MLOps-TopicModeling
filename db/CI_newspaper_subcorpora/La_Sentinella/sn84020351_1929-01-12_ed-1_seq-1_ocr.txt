One of the greatest symbols of Pan-American union will be
the coast of the Dominican Republic by the joint efforts of the 21 '
American republics. First conceived years ago, shortly after the
remains of Columbus were found reposing in the cathedral of
Santo Domingo, the idea advanced to reality last September first,
when the international contest for an architect's design of the
lighthouse opened.
The first stage of this contest will close on April 1, when the
ten best designs will be selected to enter another more exacting
fitting tribute to the
discoverer of the New World.
Preliminary requirements show that it will be a magnificent
memorial. It will be so elevated that its base can be seen above
neighboring trees. The architects are requested to produce
something extraordinary, and are reminded that mere academic
perfection will not produce a winning design. They must present
the achievements of the great historical figure in their significance
in the evolution of the world civilization.
It is a task for genius. No one possessing it can complain of
a mediocre subject on which to exercise his talents. He has an
opportunity for world poetry.
When completed, the memorial will be one of the shrines of
the New World. Situated on the coast of a West Indian island, it
will be the tribute of all American nations to one who inaugurated
American history. That all these nations should work together
in this aim is a remarkable example of their unity.
That unity is even more definitely exemplified in that portion
of the specifications which calls for the designing of a large
airport adjacent to the memorial, with an explanation that Santo
Domingo would be the half-way stop in an air route from Montreal
to Buenos Aires.
That look into the future explains why such a memorial by
all nations of the New World is possible. The day, is fast coming
when communication and social and trade intercourse will render
the distinctions between these nations negligible. It is fitting that,
cn the eve of the great Pan-America, they should unite in such a
splendid memorial to the man, who, after allegiance to the flags
of three nations, became the first American. ."..
