BRIDGEPORT STORE WINDOWS
Store windows make the holidays. Bridgeport merchants have
contributed greatly to the Yuletide spirit this year. The down-town
store windows are very effectively trimmed with Christmas
merchandise and is well worth your time to come down and view.
This matter of window dressing has become tremenduously
important. Where windows are attractive, the persons that pass
by "are made gayer, more alive by looking at them. They provide
inspiration. They awaken ambition. Streets with dull show
windows seem dead. Streets with windows trimmed tastefully,
well lighted, have charm and attract. This charm in turn is
reflected in hundreds of homes the homes of purchasers who
have been lured by an attractive window.
Bridgeport merchants have played an important part this
year in making their show windows and stores more colorful and
brighter. It isn't necessary to leave Bridgeport to do your holiday
chopping. Come down town, look around, and you'll get the spirit
and realize that right here in Bridgeport you'll find as good assort
ments as well displayed as you'll find anywhere.
CHRISTMAS TREES
The perennial question as to whether it is not a shocking
waste of our timber resources to cut down hundreds of thousands
of evergreens for Christmas trees is again being discussed.
And once more the American Tree Association defends the
use of young evergreens for Yuletide ornamental purposes and
advices those who are protesting against the alleged waste to
direct their energy to educating the people as to the proper use of
forests. The Association's position on the matter is that taken by
ail conservationists who hold that conservation does not mean
that there shall be a curtailment of the legitimate use of our
resources but that wasteful exploitation shall be stopped. Christ
mas trees are a legitimate of our forest resouces.
Some of those who are objecting to the cutting down of ever
green for this purpose are letting their emotions get the better of
their common sense. It is assumed by those who are opposing
Christmas trees that if they were not cut down all would grow
into tall Umber suitable for saw-logs. But the assumption is uri:
warranted. Both when planted by man and by nature evergreens
spring up in thick clusters,, so close together that there is no room,
for all to reach maturity. Some must yield if others are to Have
enough space and light. Foresters accordingly deliberately thin
out by cutting some of the small trees to make room for those
that are to allowed to grow. Trees thus removed may be used
f or Christmas purposes. If they were not removed they would
probably be killed in the struggle for existence with their
neighbors.
But even if all the Christmas trees would grow up if left
undisturbed, the cutting of them would be still justified because
òf the pleasure they give children. After all the loss of our timber
resources thru Christma.s trees is infinitesimal as compared 'With.
the damage done tò bur forests by fire and insects.

WHAT DO YOUR CHILDREN DO AT NIGHT?
Recently in several different parts of our nation there have
been reported misdemeanors on the parts of boys who go about
breaking into garages and cutting tires and strewing filth inside
the garage. The question arises, what is to be done about this
matter?
Another question might be asked at this juncture: Why are
those children permitted to run free and destroy property at
nights, especially during the school year? Have they no homes?
Do their parents know where they are?
It would be impossible for the parents to follow the boys
a round during the evenings, if they are permitted to go out to
play. But the parents can know with whom they are playing and
what natural amusement tendencies of these companions are. Here
it is that parents of ten fall down on their job.
They will say when asked where Willie is; "Oh! he is out
playing with Mickey McGuire but goodness only knows what they
aie into. I'm sure I don't." The thing for the parents to do is to
find out about the child and if the Mickey person is a bad charact
;i then keep their son away from him.
When boys are at certain age they sometimes are easily led to
ave a disrespect for property and property owners, if they are
iot directed in a law-abiding course. The thing for the parents to
io is to give their children sound advice, watch their impressions
rad instruct them in a such a manner that they will be good honest
rnd upright citizens:
A good method to overcome any laxity of the children's
character is to keep them in of evenings and see to it that they
ire given some profitable work to do. Do not let them run loose
uid have hours of idle time on their hands. If they have been to
school and worked and plaj'ed all day, they can easily afford to
pend a few minutes in the evening preparing their lessons for the
next day.
, We do not suggest that the parents become jailers but we do
suggest that they watch their children and their- children's
companions.
CURES FOR THE BLUES
Work is the greatest of all cures for discontentment. If you
are blue, if you feel that you haven't a friend on earth, if you
feel that you are not getting- what belongs to you, if you are-
f pending restless nights, go to work and watch the difference.
It won't be long before you will begin to feel different, you can
sleep better at night, you can digest your food better and you will
soon feel and breathe a more soothing atmosphere. Work is a
necessary adjunct to every happy and prosperous community.
