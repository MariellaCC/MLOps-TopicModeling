THE SPRING QUEST
Once more is drawing near that time of year when many an
individual eason to regret that he or she has not completed
a course in memory training.
Twice a year every household undergoes a seasonal readjust
ment. In autumn summer things are packed away and winter
things come out ; in the spring winter things are packed away and
summer things are again put into use.
Now, all careful people are particular to put things away
just where they can reach them. When they put them away they
know just where everything is, but six or eight months later the
question is, "Where?"
Soon will begin the great vernal hunt for the screws that
hold the screen doors on their hinges, the springs for said doors,
the screws and tackle for the awnings, the oil can for oiling the
lawn mower, the garden tools and left-over seeds from last year's
planting. Everything is somewhere, but that some place has a
tantalizing habit of being the last place one would think to look.
Householders are at fault in that they do not realize the
gigantic task involved in putting things away and taking them out.
One doubts that there is an efficient enough memory course to
--olve the universal problem, and there are just as grave doubts
that there is an office index system with enough cross index to
end this exasperating game of hide and seek.
THE HOME MERCHANT
The people are going to patronize the store where they think
they are getting the best values for their money. To survive in
this modern business age, the home merchant must keep-awake
and be as progressive as the chain store if he is to survive. He
must clean up, paint up, and above all learn a good bit of polite
ness. The average merchant is so indifferent when you make a
purchase that he seems very much displeased when you don't have
the correct change, never thanking you or saying, "We appreciate
your business." Why don't they offer attractive suggestions,
also attractive prices, these being special inducements to get the
housewife to trade 100 per cent with them. The merchant must
carry a complete line of nationally known, advertised products in
stead of buying the cheapest quality and getting wartime prices
for them. They can broadcast and raise all the money to fight
their competitors with but unless they really do something them
selves, they will still be in the same old rut wondering why they
don't get the business. .
