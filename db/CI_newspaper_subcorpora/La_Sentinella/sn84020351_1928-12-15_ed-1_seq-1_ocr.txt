would be much lower, our pleaasure of living would -be very
greatly lessened, and we should all be caused a great deal of
inconvenience in the matter of shopping.
It seems evident that we 'owe a certain patronage to these

duty or obligation. They are keenly aware of the necessity of
competing with metropolitan stores and have laid in Chrictmas

you endure an exhausting trip to the big city, take an hour or

CHRISTMAS
Even the humble home wants soma decoration for Christmas,
The folks do not feel it right to pass this day of sweet rejoying,
without the beauty of the green woodlands to brighten up the
commonplaces of daily life. ,
It used to be a great stunt for the young people to go out into
the woodlands and gather evergreen foliage for church decorat
ions. Many happy hours were spent in those country scenes, while
the young people gathered the pretty green stuff and frolicked
and flirt d. . , ;
- Today if we go out into woodlands for decorations, some land
owner may charge us with trespassing, and perhaps get our auto
mobile license away from us. It is a lovely thing to decorate our
homes, but all who respect the rights of landowners, and they
should not exterminate the beautiful plants which help create the
attractiveness of our state.

I protest violently and vehemently against the most destruct

of the death and tortures of others or of the rare and terrible
details of abnormal deaths, crimes and sexual perversions: '
But I protest violently against this vital error for I believe
every human being is fundamentally good at heart and that every
person is capable of being moved deeply by kindness, love, beauty,
ruth and justice if these sentiments are stimulated by the proper
p.ethods. I grant that it is not easy to write popularly "on these
ubjects but it can be done arid it is a duty which the press owes
-o the public.

bread (I refer to the bread of the spirit which we all so sorely
ìeed). Would this sort of newspaper sell? Decidedly so! Why not?
If the great thruts and beauties of morality science, art, religion
and philosophy could be properly treated and dressed up in
fascinating popular from we would have enough thrilling and
inspiring news to last us forever in addition to the ordinary and
really important events of the day. A newspaper founded on this
sound basis could not. fail to entertain the masses infinitely more
than does the dreadful and wretched entartainment which some of
;iir papers try to furnish to'day. We are fostering crime sexual
