OUR COMMUNITY IN 1930
Let us not forget our own community in 1930. We should
make the new year a time when every effort is exerted for the
benefit of our city and surrounding: territory. This can be done if
every individual will devote some time to constructive work for
the community.
It is an easy thing for one to hope for better things. It is a
difficult task for one to get out and do the work which is neces
sary to do. Many people complain about this or that being: wrong
here. How many of these people will be found in the crowd which
is trying to do things for the community?
We some times think other communities are more fortunate
in getting desirable things than we are. They are more fortunate
only to the extent that they get out and work for what they get.
We can get anything we want for our community if we engage in
more work and less idle talk. Complaining about what other
communities get that we do not get will not do anything of a
worth-while nature. If we want to experience progress next year
we will have to work and do a lot of it. Incidentally it may as well
be admitted that many communities in this section of the country
are getting busy, working for things for their community, and
their efforts are being crowned with success. Whether we make
any desiderable progress in 1930 depends solely on us. If, a year
hence, we find ourselves where we are today, we need blame no
one but ourselves. A self-satisfied attitude with what we have
and what we are doing will not get us anything. Community think
ing, baked up with honest to goodness community work, will get
us some place. It is up to us, What will your individual answef be?

IDEALS
In ancient times, the qualities which made a monarch mighty
were the characteristics of the barbarian; ferocity, physical
strength and leadership. But the savage had no ideals; his art
was crude; his ideas base and groveling. When the ruler became
a degenerate his empire crumbled! The spirit of credulity
pervaded ancient times ; the earlier religions consisted in the
worship of stones and of the forces of nature.
However, man gradually subdued the superstitions of his
mind ; crude ideas vaniched ; superb cathedrals and great univers
ities were built. Material comfort and prosperity increased. Gun
Dowder destroyed the institution of Feudalism; printing was
discovered and the "world was flooded with literature.
If we peer into past, we will discover that ideals have been
the stimulating forces of civilization. Every man must purse an
ideal. Liberty is an ideal for which man fought in 1776 and 17$9
to gain. Ideals give vigor and determination to man. They gave
rise to the glorious renaissance. Universal peace is an ideal.
Aren't the nations of the world endeavoring, through the naval
disarmament conferences, to attain it?
Washington and Lincoln represent great ideals. One gave us.
democracy and the other equality. Ideals teach us rectitude, truth
and goodness. Yes, men pass away; empires rise and crash;
monuments crumble into dust but ideals live through the ages!
Go through life with an ideal! (Arthur Cortese).
