Circa un migliaio di connazionali accorsero

è tuttora, una fucina di coscienza e di carattere,

nazionali hanno imparato i primi elementi della
nuova lingua, dei nuovi costumi, delle nuove idee
di questa grande Patria di adozione.

quarto di secolo di vita è stata un focolare di edu
cazione, di carità, di patriottismo.
Con questi auspici sublimi, la cerimonia di
Domenica non poteva riuscire che una solenne ed
austera manifestazione, resa più importante e più
autorevole con la presenza del Dr. William Horace
Day, capo della United Church della nostra città,

giorni di oggi.
E la storia è tutto un poema di fede, di lavoro,
di sacrifizio, di cui possono andare orgogliosi non
solo gli Evangelici, ma tutti gli Italiani immigrati
che nella nuova patria fanno oggetto delle loro
migliori aspirazioni anche il trionfo e il prestigio

"The life of truth is ròld an
so far mournful; but it is not
the slave of tears, contritions,
and perturbations."
Experience begins and ends with the individual no matter how
casual the beginning.
Experience is not met on the roadside. It is created and
born in the soul.
Persons and things are often involved in different exper
iences but they do not reach to the soul: The soul sets the
vibrations working on, outside things but they in turn are dead
unless animated by it.
No force can mar the soul, no matter how cruel and loathsome
the waves which reach the soul, it can only benefited if rightly
interpreted.
Often because our attention is focused on the subordinated
elements consisting of persons, things and places, we forget the
Mainspring and say-that the experiences was wished on us by
God, Fate or the Devil.
Born in us is the boundless magnet of experience.
A . smile, a tear, a careless move, a false perception perhaps,
coming from the soul aligths upon outside elements and persons
and starts casually enough - an experience.
A young man upon meeting a young woman decides that she
is the Ideal which for many years he has been longing for. After
he has been engaged he discovers that he has been too quick about
it. Nevertheless he keeps loving the girl dreaming of the day when
she will become his bride. Unexpectedly this girl, the Ideal of
purity -and honesty marries another for comfort while still pre
tending to love him. His soul rebels. What a trick! What is he to
do? Lose faith in all women? Turn from the High Way? Become
a cynic ? Then softly a Voice whispers :
Be still sad heart ; that woman has done nothing to you. Your
soul is free if you will but listen. You planted the seed of this ex
perience the evening you smilel upon her now reap the fruit.
A voice tried to speak to you then but you would not listen now
you must if want to be saved. Do not lose faith. What is one ex
perience compared to the thousands which are to follow ? "Your
fate is the common fate of all."
Thus what seems a shock is in reality a gentle; tap to real
ization. - ;
Emerson says that Divinity is behind our failures and follies
also. From this statement, we draw two facts: One is that the
Mainspring with all its tendencies and insticts which determines
our experience is born from the all Good; the other is that it is
always present in all our experiences.
It may not be very consoling to believe that whatever happens
is caused by u& rather than the Devil, God, or Fortune; life in
time of trouble may seem "cold and mournful"" but. at least we
shall be free and not "slaves of tears, contritions, and perturb
ations." There are few favorites of the Gods; most of us are pupils
some advanced others farther back. It does not help to fight or
revenge.
Let's be still. -
Return to solitude and there find not only peace but a Voice
that cries : Onward, onward, trust and do not be afraid.
The Soul is; nothing else is.
- Lucia Stella Marinaro.

A GOOD PLACE TO LIVE
Work for and your home town. Beautify it. Improve it. Make
it attractive. Help make it the kind of a town that you really want
to live in. ,
The best advertisement of your business is the town you live
in if it's the kind of a town it should be.
Cities get reputation as well as men. Repetition makes rep
utation. Make your city's reputation one that will attract favorable
attention. It will thus draw people and where people come there
is prosperity. , -
Help rid Bridgeport of one eyesore after another. Clean up
vacant lots, and plant them in gardens. Make a cluttered up yard
a disgrace and an unpainted house a reproch. Cut out the weeds, .
clean the street and fix the sidewalks. Wash the store windows
and discountenance the flaring sale sign. Be fare to your public
officials and pay your taxes without grumbling so that they may
give the best service possible back to you.
Make public opinion too hot for those who knock, refuse to
co-operate for general good and who will not help.
Our city should be one where folks are friendly, where pro- .
gressiveness is appreciated, where proper sanitary methods are
conducive to healthf ulness, where attractiveness is, considered an f
asset, where education fits for the future, where recreations are
plentiful for all, where government is. efficient and popular, where
living conditions are good, where the helping hand is extended to
the unfortunate, where employment is plentiful, where invest
ments are sound, where intolerance is unknown, and church spires
point the way to Heaven.
That's the kind of a home town we want!
IF NEWSPAPER STOPPED
No paper on the doorsteps. Newstands unnaturally bare. Car
riders wathching familiar landscapes as their trains rush cityward,
instead of renewing their contacts with the happinings of the four
continents. The world seeming strangely out of joint. Store sales
sagging abruptly for only the show windows remain to tell the
daily bargains. Finance, always dependent on the latest news,
thrown back upon the thin stream of information trickling through
the "tiker tape". A city cut off mentally from the ouside world.
Twenty-eight years before George Washington was born the
first American newspaper was established. Increasingly, during
the 200-odd years that have intervened, the newspaper has become
a matter-of course in the American home. In this day and age
one can scarcely conceive a world without newspapers. They are
a very part of the fabric of daily life, universally in demand a
running record of world events, human achievements, pathos,
tragedy, scientific progress every conceivable activity.
