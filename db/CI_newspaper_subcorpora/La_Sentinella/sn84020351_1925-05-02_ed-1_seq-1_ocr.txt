WAKE UP!
Most people resist waking up. They yawn, turn over, cover
up, and try to stay asleep. No matter what the hour, they are
not quite ready to wake up. They look daggers at the alarm
clock, and curse the sun for shining in through the window. And
yet,' waking up is a precious 'experience. There's nothing else
like it. To come out of darkness, into light, out of dormacy into
consciousness, out of inertia into activity.
Everybody should look forward to waking up not only out
of sleep, but out of ignorance, bad habits, laziness, discourage
ment and many other things. Such awakening can take place all
day long every day.
Don't resist it with a yawn, don't -turn away from it, and
don't try to cover up. Be "ready for it. Be willing to learn, to be
activeto go ahead, and to be a credit to those who care for you.
SMASH-UP SEASON.
Motoring weather is here.
And so is the season for grade crossing accidents.
The average person is awed by such disasters as the recent
Illinois tornado, yet gives but scant attention to grade crossing
dangers.
Yet almost three times as many persons were killed in grade
crossing accidents in 1924 as were killed by the tornado. A total
of 2149 lost their lives in "mixups between trains and automo
biles. We can do nothing to prevent storms. And what we do to
prevent crossing accidents is all to little, largely becausé, being
scattered it is difficult to center the public mind lipon them.
